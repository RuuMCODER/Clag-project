Night Lights

Stars are celestial bodies of burning gas millions of light years away from our very own planet earth. The Milky Way Galaxy, our home in the universe, contains billions of stars and the Sun is the closest star to Earth. There are millions of galaxies as well and the Milky Way Galaxy is only one of them. Stars vary greatly from each other in brightness, colour, size and age, put simply stars come in every form and each one is unique. The distance between our planet and these astronomical objects is measured in light years; the time it takes for the light of the star to reach us and since their so far away it takes years for that light to be visible on Earth. Stars are literally astronomical objects burning up and that burn is the light we see here on Earth. These celestial beings light up the heavens from dusk till dawn 365 times every year. Stars illuminate the nightsky in a million and one colours.

Milky Way Galaxy

A galaxy is a massive grouping or collection of stars and dust held together by gravity. Earth is found in the Milky Way Galaxy and with it are billions of other stars, planets, dwarf planets and other celestial bodies. The Milky Way is in a barred spiral and scientists believe that it even has an invisible halo. The dust does look somewhat milky if i do say so myself. The galaxy has a band of light described by many as a river, as milk and as a path and many others so one can easily guess how the name 'Milky Way' came about.
